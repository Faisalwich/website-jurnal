I think we’re both just burned out.

I’ve lost count of how many times I’ve looked at a long text I wrote for you, only to delete the whole thing. It’s not that I don’t have anything to say, it’s just that I know your head is already a mess. I know why you’ve built those walls so high, and I know how much it hurts to keep them up. I know "home" isn't exactly the place where you feel safe—if anything, it’s the place you’re most desperate to get away from, even when you're sitting right there.

The worst part is knowing I can’t do a single thing about it. I’m still stuck in this same spot; still struggling with my final semester, still looking for a job, and constantly feeling like I’m standing still while everyone else is moving on. How am I supposed to be the one you lean on when I’m already about to crack under the weight of my own family’s expectations and the anger I feel toward myself?

So, maybe "finding another time" is the most honest thing I can say right now.

I’m not giving up on us. I just need to get my life together first. I want to get to a point where I don't feel like I have to hide my own needs just because I feel "not enough." I want to be someone who actually brings you peace, not just another person drowning right next to you.

It’s okay if we don’t carry each other’s burdens today. It’s okay to just focus on surviving.

One day, the noise in our heads will finally stop, and I won't have to delete my messages anymore. Until then, I’ll be right here, dealing with my own mess—hoping that when that "other time" finally comes, I’ll actually be the man you deserve.
