I feel like I’m stuck at a red light that refuses to change.

Everywhere I look, the world is in motion. People are moving forward, chasing their careers, building their lives, and hitting their milestones. And then there’s me—standing still at this intersection, watching the lights, waiting for my turn that never seems to come.

It’s a suffocating kind of stillness. It’s the feeling of being "stuck" in a house that doesn't feel like home, trapped in a final semester that feels like a dead end, and anchored down by responsibilities that were never meant to be mine alone. I’m revving my engine, ready to take off, but the world tells me to wait. My mind tells me to run, but my reality pulls the brakes.

What do you do when you’re in the "red lights" of life? You just sit there with the noise of your own thoughts. You stare at the countdown, hoping the next second is the one where you finally get to move. You feel the heat of the sun, the frustration in your chest, and that nagging whisper that says you’re falling behind.

But maybe the red light is the only thing keeping me from a total crash.

Maybe this forced stop is where I have to learn how to breathe again, even when the air in this house feels thin. I’m out here, trusting God with the timing, even when I’m tired of waiting. I’m just waiting for that one flash of green—that one job offer, that graduation day, that moment I can finally pack my bags and build a place where I actually belong.

Until then, I’ll just keep the engine idling. I’m not turning it off. I’m just waiting for my turn to fly.

*"Tak apa, kita cari waktu lain."*

Because eventually, every light has to change.
